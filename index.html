Introduction

This website collects the insights from a project by a group of five students exploring how a master’s thesis can become a startup.

We looked into key topics such as funding opportunities, writing a business plan, and support programs like the HSLU Smart-up initiative. We also interviewed startup founders and attended three startup events to gather practical insights.

The goal of this site is to bring together the knowledge, resources, and experiences we discovered along the way.

Tipps:

  • Start with simple prototypes and test early.
  • Involve users and clients through continuous feedback.
  • Use AI as both a creative and practical support tool.
  • Start small and fund your idea through services.
  • Don’t market a prototype as a finished product.
  • Focus on endurance - it’s a marathon, not a sprint.
  • Be patient and launch only when your product is ready.
  • Be careful with spending, especially in the beginning.
  • Don’t overwork - protect your health and energy.
  • Build business skills alongside your creative work.
  • Stay realistic: a startup should eventually pay the bills.
  • Start small, dream big.
  • Invest your own money.
  • Learn to embrace uncertainty.
  • Learners are readers.
  • Inspiration can come from anywhere.
  • Trust your intuition.
  • Build relationships.
  • Step outside your comfort zone every day.
  • Work should feel meaningful, not like a chore.
